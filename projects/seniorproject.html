Blog Entries

Week 1

August 5th to 9th

8:00 AM. Today, I began my first day of the internship listening to a discussion of a future project. I got to see firsthand how professionals communicate and exchange ideas.


Afterwards, I began working on my first project. There is an online service called cloudes where users are able to create simulations of processes using the queuing paradigm. However, as it is all visual-based, it is not readily accessible to the visually impaired. So, I familiarized myself with the application and brainstormed ways to create this physically using a 3D-printer. This design needs to be recognizable to the visually impaired and the computer, which will utilize computer vision (CV) to translate it into a digital simulation.


This task presents many layers of complexities. The design needs to be both functional and aesthetic. It needs to be large enough to be recognizable by the computer while also being compact. The task seemed straight forward at first, but formulating a solution that adheres to the constraints seem quite difficult.


The following day, I continued designing. I created a PowerPoint presentation that showcases each piece and my thought-process for designing it in that way. I presented it to my mentor, Dr. Padilla, as well as another faculty member, on Friday.


I had gotten more accustomed to presenting in school, but this was a unique experience. Some constructive criticism included introducing the problem and solution at the beginning of my presentation, rather than diving into the solution. Another critique was to support design choices with research literature.


While working on this project, I got to observe a presentation by a fourth-year student on insider threats in corporations. While this does not pertain to my projects, it still offered interesting perspectives in cybersecurity and corporate ethics.

Week 2

August 12th to 16th

I further refined my designs and came up with dimensions for each piece. On Monday, I began playing with another web application called Sketchup, where I will be building the 3D models. By the end of Wednesday, I had completed most of the pieces. I had great difficulty creating the braille for the number chips. I continued to tackle this problem the next day. Eventually, I found that I could just import a component from the web.


I came on Thursday instead this week to meet Hector, who gave me a tutorial on using their 3D-printer. I did a test print with one of my designed pieces and found that the chips were slightly too big for the slots in the pieces. So, I fixed that before setting up a print overnight.


Finally printing the pieces really refreshed my motivation. Also, Dr. Padilla asked me to see another professional after this summer internship to present my design. In addition, he asked me if I would be willing to be a lead in publishing a paper on this project to present at a conference at George Mason University sometime next year.
